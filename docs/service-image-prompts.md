# Service Image Prompt Pack

These prompts are prepared for the service cards in `src/pages/ServicesPage.tsx`.

Recommended output:
- Format: `webp`
- Aspect ratio: `4:3`
- Resolution: `1600x1200` or higher
- Style: realistic industrial photography
- Usage: service card hero image
- Constraints for all images: no text, no watermark, no logo overlays, no infographic styling, no exaggerated sci-fi effects, no cartoon look

Recommended filenames:
- `surface-preparation-generated.webp`
- `surface-treatment-generated.webp`
- `piping-systems-generated.webp`
- `structural-steel-generated.webp`
- `industrial-insulation-generated.webp`
- `scaffolding-generated.webp`
- `electromechanical-generated.webp`
- `operational-support-generated.webp`

## 01. Surface Preparation

Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: service card hero image
Primary request: industrial surface preparation work on a large steel structure before coating
Scene/backdrop: active heavy industrial yard or refinery maintenance zone
Subject: technicians performing abrasive blasting on a steel tank or structural steel section
Style/medium: high-end realistic industrial photography
Composition/framing: medium-wide shot, clear subject focus, visible blasting hose and prepared steel surface, cinematic but practical framing
Lighting/mood: natural daylight with controlled grit and atmosphere
Color palette: steel gray, off-white dust, muted industrial blue, safety gear accents
Materials/textures: rough blasted steel, dust, hoses, PPE, industrial scaffolding in background
Constraints: show real surface preparation activity, not painting; workers should wear realistic PPE
Avoid: sparks, welding, exaggerated smoke, empty yard with no clear task

## Notes For Generation

- Keep the imagery grounded in real heavy industry, shipyard, refinery, or power-generation environments.
- Prioritize Saudi or Gulf industrial visual language where possible: arid light, large-scale energy infrastructure, refinery/process plant context, disciplined PPE and engineering realism.
- Avoid stock-photo clichés and overdramatic HDR treatment.
- Keep each image distinct so the services do not visually blur together.
